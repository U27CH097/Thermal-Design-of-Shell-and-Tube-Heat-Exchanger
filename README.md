# Thermal-Design-of-Shell-and-Tube-Heat-Exchanger
project involved the thermal design of a shell-and-tube heat exchanger for cooling methanol condensate using brackish water as the cooling medium.

The objective was to cool 100,000 kg/h of methanol condensate from 95°C to 40°C, while the cooling water entered at 25°C and left at 40°C.

I started by performing a heat balance to calculate the required heat duty, which was approximately 4.34 MW. Using this heat duty, I determined the required cooling water flow rate.
Next, I estimated an initial overall heat transfer coefficient (U) of 600 W/m²·K based on standard design data and calculated the Log Mean Temperature Difference (LMTD). Since I selected a 1-shell-pass and 2-tube-pass exchanger, I applied the LMTD correction factor (F) to obtain the true temperature difference.
Using the heat transfer equation, Q=UAΔT

I calculated the required heat transfer area to be approximately 289.5 m².

I then selected the exchanger geometry by choosing 20 mm outer diameter tubes, 16 mm inner diameter, 4.88 m tube length, and a triangular tube layout. Based on the required area, I calculated that 954 tubes were needed, resulting in a shell diameter of approximately 909 mm.

After finalizing the geometry, I calculated the tube-side and shell-side heat transfer coefficients using Reynolds number, Prandtl number, and Colburn j-factor correlations. The calculated heat transfer coefficients were approximately 3738 W/m²·K on the tube side and 2014 W/m²·K on the shell side.

I also included tube wall resistance and fouling factors to calculate the final overall heat transfer coefficient, which came out to be approximately 689 W/m²·K. This differed from the initially assumed value by only 14.9%, indicating that the design was acceptable.

Finally, I calculated the tube-side pressure drop (6.8 kPa) and shell-side pressure drop (70 kPa) to ensure they were within the allowable design limits.

The final outcome was a complete preliminary thermal design of a shell-and-tube heat exchanger, including heat duty, exchanger sizing, heat transfer analysis, pressure drop calculations, and design validation
